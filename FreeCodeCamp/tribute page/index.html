W.E.B DuBois constructing the foundation of the NAACP

What is the NAACP?

The vision of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People is to ensure a society in which all individuals have equal rights without discrimination based on race.

Here's a time line of W.E.B DuBois Life

  • 1868 - William Edward burghardt Du Bois was born in Great Barrington, Massachusetts
  • 1895 - Du Bois becomes the first African American to earn a Ph.D from Harvard University.
  • 1906 - Du Bois, Along with Minnesota attorney Fredrick L. McGhee and others, help found the Niagara Movement with William Monroe Trotter.
  • 1909 - DuBois Shares founding of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP).
  • 1961 - Du Bois was invited to Ghana in 1961 by President Kwame Nkrumah to direct the Encyclopedia Africana, a goverment production ,and a long-held dream of his. When in 1963, he was refused a new U.S passport, he and his wife, shirley Graham Du Bois, became Citizens of Ghana.
  • 1963 W.E.B Du Bois dies August 27, 1963
